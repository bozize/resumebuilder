Resume Writing Tips & Career Advice

Expert guidance to help you create a resume that gets you noticed by employers

1. Tailor Your Resume for Each Job

Targeted Applications

Customize your resume for each position by highlighting the most relevant skills and experiences. Use keywords from the job description to get past Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) and show you're a perfect fit.

2. Start with a Strong Summary

First Impressions Matter

Begin with a professional summary that highlights your key qualifications and career goals. Keep it concise (3-4 sentences) and focus on what you can offer the employer.

3. Quantify Your Achievements

Show Impact

Use numbers and metrics to demonstrate your accomplishments. For example: "Increased sales by 30%" or "Reduced processing time by 25%." This helps employers understand the impact you can make.

4. Keep It Clean and Professional

Design Matters

Use a clean, easy-to-read format with consistent formatting. Stick to professional fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman, and keep the font size between 10-12pt. Our resume templates are designed with these principles in mind.

5. Highlight Relevant Skills

Skills That Matter

Create a dedicated skills section that matches the job requirements. Include both hard skills (e.g., software proficiency) and soft skills (e.g., leadership, communication).

6. Proofread Thoroughly

Avoid Costly Mistakes

Typos and grammatical errors can quickly eliminate you from consideration. Proofread your resume multiple times, and consider having someone else review it as well.
